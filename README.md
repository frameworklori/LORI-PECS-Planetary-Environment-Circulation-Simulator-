# Atmosphere-Retention-vs-Magnetic-Shield-Test

> Does a magnetic field preserve an atmosphere — or is it only one part of a larger system?

---

## 🌍 Overview

This project investigates how atmospheres are formed, sustained, and lost under different planetary conditions.

Rather than assuming magnetic fields are the primary factor, this framework explores **atmospheric retention as a system-level balance** between gas supply and multiple loss mechanisms.

The goal is to determine whether magnetic shielding alone can preserve an atmosphere, or whether it acts only as a secondary modifier within a broader physical system.

---

## 🧠 Core Hypothesis

A magnetic field alone is not sufficient to sustain an atmosphere.

Atmospheric stability emerges from the interaction between gravity, temperature, volatile supply, and loss processes.

---

## 🔑 Core Principle

An atmosphere exists only if:

gas supply ≥ total gas loss

---

## ⚙️ Atmospheric Loss Model (Minimal Form)

Total loss is simplified as:

Loss = Thermal Escape + Solar Wind Loss

Where:

- Thermal Escape ≈ f(temperature, gravity)
- Solar Wind Loss ≈ f(magnetic_field, atmospheric_density)

This project uses a reduced model to isolate key variables while allowing controlled expansion into more complex physics.

---

## 🔬 Key Insight

Magnetic fields do not create atmospheres.

They modify the interaction between a planet and its space environment.

In some conditions, magnetic fields reduce atmospheric loss.  
In others, they may channel energy and enhance ion escape.

The dominant controlling factors remain:

- Gravity (escape velocity)
- Temperature (thermal energy)
- Sustained volatile supply
